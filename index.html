History

National History

alpha Kappa Delta Phi was established at the University of California at Berkeley in the fall of 1989, and recognized by the College Panhellenic Association on February 7, 1990. The fourteen founding sisters dedicated themselves to establishing a strong and lasting organization, which would provide Asian American women the opportunity to participate in the Greek system. Soon after being recognized by the College Panhellenic Association, the founding sisters brought together a diverse group of women who shared with them the goals of sisterhood, scholarship, and leadership. These ideals were to later set the foundation for alpha Kappa Delta Phi’s purpose.

The love and generosity that each member had for the organization was instrumental to the growth of alpha Kappa Delta Phi's reputation as a sorority, as well as to the forging of unbreakable friendships. The sisterhood has since then spread to prominent university campuses across the country. To meet the needs of the growing sisterhood, the National Board of alpha Kappa Delta Phi was established in May of 1993 at the National Convention in Santa Barbara, California.

It is the sincere wish of the founding sisters that alpha Kappa Delta Phi continue to promote Asian Awareness in the community and leadership for women by providing each woman with the resources, unique sisterly support and understanding to achieve her goals in life.

Chapter History

On May 13, 2006, the 41st chapter of alpha Kappa Delta Phi was established by seven sophomores (Duke '08) and six freshmen (Duke '09), aided by their host chapter, the Johns Hopkins University, and their intake mom, Donna Wang Su of University of California, Santa Barbara. On May 29, 2011, Duke University alpha Kappa Delta Phi achieved Associate Chapter status at the National Convention in Buffalo, New York. On May 24, 2014, Duke University alpha Kappa Delta Phi achieved Full-Fledged Chapter status as the Alpha Beta Chapter of alpha Kappa Delta Phi at the National Convention in Orlando, Florida. As a group dedicated to the sorority's values of sisterhood, scholarship, leadership, service, and Asian awareness, our sisters are active participants in a wide variety of campus activities, such as Duke Asian Student Association, Duke Smart Women Securities, an organization for women with an interest in business and finance, and Health Arts Network at Duke, a volunteer program to bring art and music performances to extended-stay patients at the Duke Hospital. In addition, a large percentage of the sisters also hold jobs or do research at the Duke Hospital, VA Hospital, and various labs on campus.

The sisters of Duke University alpha Kappa Delta Phi are equally dedicated to our national philanthropy, breast cancer awareness. Each year, more than 240,000 women and 1,000 men will be diagnosed with breast cancer in the United States - that's one person every three minutes. We support the Avon Breast Cancer Crusade to raise funds and awareness for breast cancer research and education.

Each year, we continue to promote our pillars on campus. Look out for our annual Iris Week, Breast Cancer Awareness Week, and an array of cultural, service, and social events!

Pillars


Sisterhood

A sorority is first and foremost a sisterhood - it's what holds us together and helps us through thick and thin. The girls in alpha Kappa Delta Phi provide a family away from home, a shoulder to cry on, and a best friend to laugh the night away with. Even when we are polar opposites, we have a mutual and profound respect for each other and for the sisterhood. The memories that we share together will stick with us for the rest of our lives, and we can’t imagine it any differently!

Scholarship

As Duke students, academics is one of our top priorities. Our sisters’ range of majors and minors is a testament to the diversity of our academic interests. Economics, photography, biomedical engineering, international comparative studies, public policy, visual arts – you name it! We’re always eager to help each other with classes and encourage each other to pursue our goals.

Asian Awareness

From sights and sounds to taste and smells, we love and celebrate Asian culture. Our annual Cultural Night Market brings the Taiwanese nightlife right to the middle of the BC Plaza! We believe that having an appreciation for multicultural backgrounds gives one the opportunity to enjoy the best of what each country has to offer. We’re strong supporters of all cultural groups and events on campus and often co-host events to strengthen Asian awareness on Duke’s campus. Keep an eye out for our Asian snacks and shaved ice events throughout the year!

Service

Our national philanthropy is the Avon Breast Cancer Crusade. Nationally, alpha Kappa Delta Phi has raised over tens of thousands of dollars per year for breast cancer awareness, and we’re proud to contribute to this sum. We celebrate Breast Cancer Awareness Month in October with an annual BCA Week featuring fundraisers, workshops, and discussions.

What is rush?

Rush lasts 1.5 to 2 weeks and gives you the chance to learn about aKDPhi and meet the sisters. From information sessions and service activities to karaoke and speed dating, we hold a variety of events to give you a sense of the sorority's pillars, values, and character. Even if you're undecided about Greek life, rush is noncommital and casual. This is your opportunity to have a great time, make new friends, and see if this sisterhood is the right fit for you! Come out to events and just have fun - all events are FREE for rushees, and there is no obligation with participating.

Who can join aKDPhi?

We celebrate all forms of diversity and welcome everyone at our rush events. According to Duke's Greek policy, freshmen need to wait until spring semester to rush any Greek organization, but upperclassmen are welcome at both our fall and spring rush events.

Why should I consider joining Duke aKDPhi?

aKDPhi is the largest Asian-American interest sorority in the world with an alumni network that connects you with thousands of sisters - a unique and valuable resource. aKDPhi gives you the chance to interact and collaborate with other organizations on campus, building social, professional, and leadership skills. Most of all, the friendships you make in aKDPhi are ones that will last a lifetime!

About aKDPhi

Alpha Kappa Delta Phi Sorority, Inc. fosters the making of successful leaders by assisting and challenging our members to reach their maximum potential. We strive to maintain the highest levels of integrity and innovation in the promotion of sisterhood, philanthropy, scholastic excellence, and Asian-Awareness in the community while creating lifelong relationships.
